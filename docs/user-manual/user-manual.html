1. Introduction — What is the PRDF LMS?

1.1 About PRDF

The People's Republic Development Fund (PRDF) is a developmental finance institution that gives South African small and medium enterprises (SMEs) access to affordable business loans — from R10,000 up to R10 million — for qualifying businesses. Beyond funding, PRDF offers non-financial support services such as business health assessments, compliance support, skills training, funding-readiness webinars, marketing & branding assistance and SARS support.

The PRDF Loan Management System (LMS) is the online platform that runs this entire lending journey. It replaces paper forms, branch visits and email back-and-forth with a fast, transparent, fully online process: a business owner can check whether they qualify, apply for funding, upload supporting documents, give the legally required consent and then track their application step by step — while PRDF staff triage, review, verify and approve those applications from a single workspace.

1.2 The two applications

ApplicationWho uses itWhat it does
Client Portal Business owners applying for funding Public landing page and loan calculator, eligibility self-assessment, account registration, a five-step application wizard, document uploads, POPIA consent capture, a live status tracker, and a view of disbursed loans.
Admin Console PRDF internal staff (interns, originators, loan officers, administrators) A role-aware workspace with a pipeline dashboard, application review and document verification, a full audit history, status and assignment management, portfolio monitoring, reports & analytics, and user access (role) management.

1.3 Who should read this manual

  • Part 1 is written for clients — business owners who want to apply for PRDF funding and track their application.
  • Part 2 is written for PRDF staff who process applications in the Admin Console.
  • The Appendix contains a status glossary, the required-documents checklist and answers to common questions — useful to both audiences.
Screens shown in this manual were captured from the live system. Names, amounts and businesses shown are demonstration data.

2. Technology Overview

2.1 What the system is built with

The PRDF LMS is a modern web application. You do not need to install anything — both the Client Portal and the Admin Console run in an ordinary web browser. Under the hood, the platform is built from the following components:

LayerTechnologyIn plain terms
User interfaces React 18 + TypeScript, built with Vite The screens you interact with. Two separate applications are built on the same foundation: the client-facing portal and the staff-facing admin console. Both are fast, responsive and work on desktop, laptop and mobile browsers.
Data, sign-in & files Supabase (PostgreSQL database, Auth, Storage) A secure cloud platform that stores all application data in a PostgreSQL database, manages user accounts and passwords, and holds uploaded documents in protected file storage.
Application API ASP.NET Core (.NET 9), Clean Architecture A Microsoft .NET server application that exposes the system's business logic (health checks, profiles, notifications, reporting). The platform can be switched between talking to Supabase directly or through this API.
Automation & operations GitHub Actions CI/CD, uptime monitoring Every change to the software is automatically built, tested and deployed, and the running system is monitored so outages are detected quickly.
Notifications SendGrid (email), Twilio (SMS) — optional providers Channels through which the system can notify applicants and staff of important events.

2.2 Security, privacy and compliance

  • POPIA-compliant consent — before any application is submitted, the client must explicitly acknowledge each data-privacy and policy item. Every answer is recorded individually against the application.
  • Role-based access control (RBAC) — staff see only what their role allows (Intern, Originator, Loan Officer, Admin), and only a Super Admin can manage other administrators.
  • Row-level security (RLS) — database rules ensure clients can only ever read their own applications, documents and loans.
  • Authenticated sessions — sign-in is handled by Supabase Auth using industry-standard JSON Web Tokens (JWT); passwords are never stored in plain text.
  • Full audit trail — every status change on an application records who made it and when. Nothing happens off the record.

2.3 What you need to use it

  • A modern web browser (Chrome, Edge, Firefox or Safari) on any device.
  • An internet connection.
  • For clients: an email address to register, and digital copies (PDF, JPG or PNG) of your supporting documents.
  • For staff: an account provisioned by a PRDF administrator with the appropriate role.

Part 1

The Client Portal

For business owners: from discovering PRDF and checking eligibility, to applying, submitting documents, giving consent and tracking your application.

3.1 The public landing page and instant loan calculator

Your journey starts on the PRDF homepage. It tells the whole story at a glance — who PRDF is, who qualifies for funding, how the process works, the non-financial support services on offer, and exactly which documents you will need, so there are no surprises later.

In the top-right corner of the hero you will find the instant loan calculator:

  1. Drag the "How much do you need?" slider to the amount you want to borrow — PRDF loans range from R250 000 to R5 million.
  2. Drag the "Over how many months?" slider to your preferred repayment term.
  3. The estimated monthly repayment, total repayment and total fees update instantly as you move the sliders.
  4. When you are happy with the numbers, click Apply Now — your chosen amount and term are carried into the application.

3.2 Checking your eligibility

Before applying, run the two-minute eligibility self-assessment. Click Check Eligibility on the homepage. The assessment lists PRDF's criteria in three groups — tick every box that applies to your business:

  • Ownership & Structure — e.g. the enterprise is >50.1% black women owned, 90% South African nationals control operations, directors are permanent SA residents.
  • Registration & Compliance — e.g. registered with the CIPC and SARS, valid tax clearance, directors are not un-rehabilitated insolvents or under debt review.
  • Business Viability & Impact — e.g. demonstrates developmental impact, targets employment creation, has the capacity to repay the loan.

The counter at the bottom shows how many of the 15 criteria you have confirmed. When all criteria are ticked, click Check My Eligibility.

If your business meets the requirements you will see an immediate "You qualify for PRDF funding" result, with a summary of each category and a single call to action: Create Account & Apply.

3.3 Creating an account and signing in

Registration takes less than two minutes. Fill in your first name, last name, phone number, email address and a password, then click Create Account & Apply. If you arrived from the loan calculator, the panel on the left confirms the amount and term you are applying for.

Returning clients simply sign in with their email address and password to pick up where they left off. Use Forgot your password? on the sign-in page if you need to reset it.

3.4 Your home dashboard

Once signed in, the portal greets you by name. The sidebar on the left is your main navigation: Home, Applications (the application wizard), Documents, My Loans and Status.

The Home dashboard summarises everything that matters:

  • Active application — your current application and its status (e.g. Under Review).
  • Counters — applications submitted, active loans and amount outstanding.
  • Recent activity — a feed of the latest updates on your applications.

3.5 Applying for funding — the five-step wizard

Click Applications in the sidebar (or Apply Now) to start. The application is a guided wizard in five steps, shown in the progress bar at the top: Business Profile → Financials → Loan Details → Documents → Review. Throughout the wizard, the Debt Breakdown panel on the right keeps your chosen loan amount, term, monthly payment, total payable and fees in view — click Edit loan details at any time to adjust them.

Step 1 — Business & Compliance Profile

Capture your business's basic details (name, industry, address, city, province), the demographic information PRDF reports on as a developmental funder (primary director gender, % South African ownership, where the business operates, ownership flags), and your compliance and registration details (CIPC registration number, SARS tax PIN and compliance declarations). Click Continue when done.

Step 2 — Financial Information

Help PRDF understand the financial health of your business: average monthly revenue, years in operation, number of employees and your business bank.

Step 3 — Loan Details

Set your preferred loan using the two sliders — how much you need (R10,000 to R10,000,000) and over how many months (1 to 60). The live calculation shows your monthly repayment, total repayment and total fees. Then choose a loan purpose category (e.g. Working Capital) and describe how you will use the funds.

Step 4 — Documents

Upload the six required supporting documents. For each one, click the upload box (or drag and drop the file). Accepted formats are PDF, JPG and PNG.

  1. ID Document — certified copy of the director's / applicant's identity document
  2. Proof of Address — recent proof of business or director address
  3. Company Registration (CIPC) — CIPC company registration certificate
  4. Tax Clearance — SARS tax clearance or tax compliance status document
  5. Bank Statements — the last 3 months of business bank statements
  6. Financial Statements — latest annual financials or management accounts

Step 5 — Review

The final step shows everything you have entered — business profile, financial information, loan details, the documents you attached and the full cost breakdown. Use Back to correct anything. When you are satisfied, click Submit Application.

3.6 Saving a draft and finishing later

You do not have to complete the application in one sitting. Click Save & finish later on any step of the wizard — your progress is saved as a draft and a confirmation appears. When you sign in again, the draft is shown on the Status page with a Resume your draft button that returns you to exactly where you stopped, with all your answers preserved.

3.7 Consent and submitting your application

When you click Submit Application, PRDF must first capture your explicit, POPIA-compliant consent. A consent form appears with two groups of items, each answered individually with YES or NO:

  • Data Privacy (POPIA) — consent for PRDF to collect, process and store your information for this application; to verify your information with credit bureaus and assess your credit worthiness; and acknowledgement that PRDF retains information for the periods required by law and that you may request access to or correction of it.
  • Policy Acknowledgements — that you understand the documentation you must submit; that submitting does not mean the application is approved; that if unsuccessful PRDF will issue a letter with the reason; and that you understand the meaning of each clause in any agreement you sign.

Answer every item, then click Proceed. Your application, its documents and the consent record are all saved together, and you are taken straight to the status tracker where the new application appears as Submitted.

3.8 Tracking your application status

Open Status in the sidebar at any time. Each application is shown with a step-by-step tracker that makes an otherwise opaque process completely transparent. Each application moves through the following stages:

Application SubmittedUnder ReviewApproved Funds DisbursedIn RepaymentLoan Closed

Completed stages show a green tick, the current stage is highlighted in blue, and each card shows the application reference, purpose and when it was last updated. Use New Application in the top-right corner to start another application.

3.9 Managing your documents

The Documents page lets you upload and track documents outside the wizard — useful when PRDF requests additional or corrected files.

  1. Choose the application from the Select application dropdown.
  2. The Required Documents checklist shows each document and whether it is still MISSING.
  3. Click Upload next to a document, drop the file into the upload panel, and click Upload Document.
  4. Files you have submitted appear under Uploaded Files.

3.10 My Loans

Once an application is approved and the funds are disbursed, the loan — with its repayment schedule and outstanding balance — appears under My Loans. Until then the page shows an empty state.

Part 2

The Admin Console

For PRDF staff: triaging applications, verifying documents, moving cases through the pipeline, monitoring the portfolio, reading analytics and managing user access.

4.1 Signing in and the Admin Dashboard

Staff sign in to the Admin Console with their PRDF account. The console is role-aware: the pages and actions you see depend on your role (Intern, Originator, Loan Officer or Admin). The sidebar gives access to Dashboard, Applications, Loans, Portfolio, Reports and — for administrators — User Access.

The dashboard is a snapshot of your pipeline:

  • Pipeline Cases — total open cases in the pipeline.
  • Under Review — cases currently being reviewed.
  • Overdue Tasks — tasks past their due date.
  • SLA Breached — flagged in amber: applications that have waited longer than the service-level agreement allows.
  • Assigned Queue — your work queue, newest first, each entry showing its reference, last update and current status.

4.2 The Applications workspace

The heart of the console is Applications — a searchable, filterable list of every case, with a tabbed case-file workspace alongside it.

  • Search by application ID or purpose.
  • Filter by status (e.g. Submitted to surface brand-new applications at the top of the funnel).
  • Each row shows the purpose, amount, status, assignee and last update — with a red SLA Breached badge where applicable.
  • Click Review to open the case in the detail workspace.

4.3 Reviewing a case — Details tab

Opening a case loads the detail workspace on the Details tab, giving the full picture: client name and contact details, business name, registration number and address, the requested amount, purpose and term, the developmental-impact demographics PRDF reports on, plus when the case was created and submitted and who it is assigned to. The other tabs — Documents, History, Tasks, Notes and Advisory (NFS) — cover the rest of the case file, including non-financial support advisory notes.

4.4 Verifying documents

The Documents tab is a verification checklist. Every file the applicant submitted — ID, proof of address, CIPC registration, tax clearance, three months of bank statements and financial statements — is listed with its upload date. For each file you can:

  • View / Download — open the file to inspect it.
  • Verify — mark the document as checked and acceptable.
  • Reject — mark it as unacceptable so the client can be asked to re-submit.

Staff can also upload a document on the client's behalf: choose the document type, drag the file in, and click Upload Document.

4.5 The audit history

The History tab is the full audit trail of the case — every status transition, who made it and exactly when. Nothing happens to an application off the record, which supports both internal governance and regulatory reporting.

4.6 Moving a case forward — status, assignment and requests

The Internal Actions panel on the Details tab is where you act on a case:

  • Assign to user — pick a colleague and click Save Assignment to route the case to them.
  • Change status — select the next status (e.g. Under Review), optionally add a status note, and click Update Status. The status badge updates instantly and the transition is logged in History.
  • Request info — describe what is missing and click Request More Info to ask the applicant for it.

4.7 The Portfolio dashboard

Once loans are disbursed, Portfolio tracks the health and exposure of the loan book: total loans, active loans, the outstanding balance, and an Arrears panel listing any overdue installments. Click Export CSV for a one-click download of the data.

4.8 Reports & analytics

The Reports workspace turns the loan book into insight, with a time-range filter at the top. It includes:

  • Pipeline Status Summary — how many applications sit at each stage.
  • Origination Volume Trend — amounts disbursed over time.
  • Pipeline Conversion and Staff Productivity metrics.
  • Audit Log — system-wide activity for the selected period.
  • Demographic Breakdown — client composition for NCR / BEE compliance reporting (gender, black women-owned, HDP, disability, rural).
  • Debtors Book Age Analysis — outstanding installments aged by days overdue.
  • Province Breakdown and Spatial Classification (rural / township / city) — the geographic reporting PRDF provides as a developmental funder.
  • Export Center — every dataset downloadable as CSV for board and regulatory reporting.

4.9 User access and roles

User Access (visible to administrators only) is where internal roles are granted and revoked. The tiles summarise visible users, admins and super admins; the list can be searched by name or email and filtered by user type or role. To grant a role, pick it in the Assign Role dropdown next to the user and click Assign.

RoleTypical use
ClientAn applicant using the Client Portal.
InternLimited internal access for support work.
OriginatorBrings in and captures new applications.
Loan OfficerReviews cases, verifies documents, moves applications through the pipeline.
AdminFull console access, including user access management.
Super AdminThe only role that can grant or revoke Admin access.

Access is least-privilege: each role sees only the pages and actions it needs.

5.3 Frequently asked questions

How much can I borrow, and over what period?
PRDF loans range from R250 000 to R5 million, repayable over 1 to 60 months. The lending rate is Prime+, up to P+8, based on the quality of the transaction — any instalment shown in the calculator is indicative until your final rate is confirmed.

Do I need to finish my application in one go?
No. Click Save & finish later on any step. Your draft appears on the Status page with a Resume your draft button.

How do I know where my application is in the process?
Open Status in the Client Portal. The tracker shows exactly which stage your application has reached, from Submitted through to Loan Closed.

What happens after I submit?
Your application is assigned to a PRDF reviewer, who verifies your documents and moves the case to Under Review. If anything is missing, PRDF will request more information. If the application is unsuccessful, PRDF issues a letter with the reason.

Is my information safe?
Yes. Your data is processed only with your recorded POPIA consent, stored in a secured cloud database with row-level security, and documents are held in protected storage that only you and authorised PRDF staff can access.

Can I apply for more than one loan?
Yes — use New Application on the Status page to start another application at any time.
